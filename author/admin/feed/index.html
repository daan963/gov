Dhaka University (DU): A cocktail explosion has been carried out at the Student-Teacher Center (TSC) Swarjit Swadhinta Chatwar. This incident happened on Wednesday (November 15) at approximately 10 pm.

After the explosion, three people were caught red-handed and handed over to the police. They are Noor Mohammad Shikder (23), Bakhtiar Chowdhury Shahin (27) and Rubel.  

It is claimed that these three have exploded the cocktail. But for what purpose they exploded it is not known.

Eyewitnesses said that there were several sounds in front of the TSC bus. After that, smoke is generated from there. They confirmed to BanglaNews that there were at least 4-5 cocktail explosions.

No one was injured in the explosion. No one was injured.

Proctor of Dhaka University confirmed this information. Maksudur Rahman told BanglaNews that there have been several cocktail explosions in the TSC area. In this incident, three people were arrested and handed over to Shahbag police station.

Sheikh Wali Asif Inan, general secretary of the Central Committee of BCL, who witnessed the incident, said, “I was going to play badminton at Hakim Chatwar when I heard two-three cocktail explosions.” I ran and saw that the students caught one while escaping on a motorcycle. Another person was later arrested as a suspect. All three have been handed over to the law enforcement agencies.

Jahangirnagar University: Progressive students of Jahangirnagar University (JABI) staged a protest march and human chain in support of the half-hour hartal called by the Left Democratic Alliance across the country to protest the announcement of the schedule for the upcoming National Assembly elections.  

On Thursday (November 16) at 11:30 a.m., they staged a protest in front of the university’s main gate (Dairy Gate) adjacent to the Dhaka-Aricha highway.

When the police came and stopped the protest, the students formed a human chain there. At this time, the presence of the leaders and activists of the university branch of the Chhatra League can be seen beside the protest march.  

Alif Mahmud, convenor (part) of the Student Union University Parliament said, ‘This university runs on public tax money. People give money because the students will come out and become bureaucrats, lawyers, politicians. They will later serve these people. Historically, this country has never had a fair election under the government. None of the 1996 elections, 2014 or 2018 elections were fair. Elections will not be fair under this fascist government. A dictator has never survived in this country. Dictatorship Ayub Khan could not survive, dictatorship Ershad could not survive this fascist government will not survive. ‘ 

General Secretary of Samajtantrik Sramik Front Savar-Ashulia Jiban Ahmed said yesterday we saw an illegal Election Commission’s undemocratic schedule announcement. This schedule declaration is completely illegal, undemocratic. We will see another election like the last 2018 election and the one before that. We do not want this election. We have always said that elections should be held under a non-partisan caretaker government. Elections have never been fair under this government. So today we stand up for my right to vote. for our survival. Give us back our right to vote. Today commodity prices rise. Our program is to bring down the syndicate of this government.  

He further said, “But the police stopped us today by blocking the start of our protest march.” We cannot be stopped. Because this movement is a fight for our survival. We are standing here for our survival.

Former General Secretary of Samajtantrik Chhatra Front, Kanoj Kanti Roy, also spoke at the human chain, organized by Jabi branch of Samajtantrik Chhatra Front, Sohagi Samia.  

However, denying the fact of obstructing the procession, Ashulia police station officer-in-charge SM Kamruzzaman said, we did not obstruct anyone. They came, they left in human chains.

Jamalpur : Bangladesh University Grants Commission (UGC) member Professor Dr. thinks that there is no alternative to new research and innovation for the development of the country. Md. Sajjad Hossain.

He said, ‘Universities should play a leading role in creating science-minded and humanitarian people in today’s technology-dependent world.The history of the university is very old. Its role in the development of mankind is also extensive. In this continuation, we also have to participate in the development of the country and the nation from our respective places. For the development of the country by using science and technology, new research and strategies have to be invented. ‘

He said these things while addressing the chief guest at the three-day national conference organized by the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) of Bangamata Sheikh Fazilatunnessa Mujib University of Science and Technology (Bashefmubiprobi) on Wednesday (November 15) morning.

In the speech of the chief guest by joining the program virtually, the country’s renowned information technology professor Dr. Md. Sajjad Hossain also said, ‘In order to create skilled manpower for building a smart Bangladesh, the curriculum of the university should be modernized by including various modern technologies including robotics and Internet of Things (IoT), Artificial Intelligence (AI), digital security, big data, blockchain. Research activities should be increased using technology, innovation should be increased. ‘ 

Pointing out that the government is working to improve the quality of life of the people and establish a knowledge-based economy through digitization, innovation and research-based education, he said, ‘If these modern technologies are added to the university curriculum, skilled manpower will be created in the country and a large amount of foreign exchange will be earned by exporting these manpower abroad. will be possible. ‘

He emphasized on increasing the knowledge and skills of the students with the aim of creating an efficient and self-reliant manpower to keep pace with the world.  

University Vice-Chancellor Professor Dr. Md. Kamrul Alam Khan presided.  

He said, ‘Hopefully, this conference will increase the power of knowledge and understanding of students. Such seminars and conferences will continue to be organized in the future for the welfare of the students. With the aim of building smart   
Bangladesh, some teams from Jagannath University, Sheikh Hasina University, Sheikh Fazilatunnecha Mujib University participated in the conference titled ‘National Conference on Robotics and IOT’. Teams showcased various projects including their own built robots. Various posters are also displayed.

Workshops were held on the first two days of the conference, which started on November 13 and 14. In the first part of the seminar held on Wednesday, the main article was first presented by Associate Professor of Robotics and Mechatronics Engineering Department of Dhaka University. Shamim Ahmed Dewan. In the next phase, the director of IMCT department of UGC. Md. Sultan Mahmud Bhuiyan presented the original article.
